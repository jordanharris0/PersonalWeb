Hobbies and Interests

Fitness

Fitness is a core part of my lifestyle, and I'm committed to maintaining consistent training through regular gym sessions and disciplined meal prep routines. I believe that the discipline and persistence required in fitness training directly translates to my approach as an engineer—both require setting clear goals, tracking progress, and pushing through challenging moments to achieve results. There's something deeply satisfying about the tangible progress you can measure in the gym, whether it's increased strength or improved endurance.

Meal prepping has become both a practical necessity and a therapeutic ritual for me, allowing me to maintain nutritional consistency while managing a demanding career at Verizon. Planning meals, cooking in bulk, and organizing nutrition forces me to think strategically about resource allocation and time management—skills that benefit both my fitness journey and professional development. The habit of preparing for the week ahead mirrors my engineering mindset of planning and executing methodically.

I view fitness as an investment in my overall well-being and mental health, not just physical appearance. The gym is where I clear my mind after complex debugging sessions and process technical challenges while working through a challenging workout. This balance between physical exertion and mental clarity has been crucial in maintaining the focus and energy required to excel in my role as an automation engineer.

Beyond the obvious health benefits, my commitment to fitness has taught me the importance of consistency, patience, and long-term thinking. These principles apply directly to my career—breakthrough achievements in both fitness and engineering come not from short bursts of effort, but from sustained dedication to continuous improvement and pushing beyond perceived limitations.

Travel

Travel has become one of my greatest passions, and I've been fortunate to explore 46 of the 50 states, along with memorable trips to Mexico and Canada. Each destination has enriched my perspective and broadened my understanding of different cultures, landscapes, and people. From the vibrant energy of major cities to the serene beauty of remote natural areas, I've learned that the world is far more diverse and complex than any geography textbook could convey.

Snowboarding is my favorite adventure activity, and I've had the privilege of riding some of North America's most iconic mountains. I've carved runs at Taos New Mexico, conquered the challenging terrain at Wolf Creek and Purgatory in Colorado, and experienced the legendary slopes of Whistler Mountain in Canada—the very venue that hosted the Olympic Winter Games. Each mountain has its own character, challenges, and rewards, and the progression of skills across different terrain has been incredibly rewarding.

Beyond snowboarding, travel has taught me adaptability and resilience in unfamiliar environments, qualities that directly benefit my technical work. Navigating new cities, solving logistical challenges, and pushing my physical limits have all contributed to personal growth and confidence. The exposure to diverse problem-solving approaches across different regions has informed how I approach software engineering challenges.

The drive to explore all 50 states and experience new landscapes continues to motivate me to plan new adventures and seek out experiences I haven't yet encountered. Travel has reminded me that there's always more to learn, discover, and appreciate in the world—a mindset that keeps me humble as an engineer and eager to embrace new technologies and methodologies in my career.

Gaming

Gaming is my ultimate escape and a passion that spans across virtually every genre and platform available. From competitive esports titles to sprawling single-player narratives, I've always believed that games offer unique experiences that rival any other form of entertainment. My all-time favorite is Rocket League—there's something incredibly satisfying about the mechanical depth, competitive integrity, and the pure skill ceiling that keeps me coming back season after season.

Action RPGs hold a special place in my heart as my favorite game genre, offering the perfect blend of challenging combat systems, rich storytelling, and character progression that keeps me engaged for hundreds of hours. The intricate mechanics and responsive gameplay required in ARPGs appeal to the same problem-solving mindset I apply to engineering. Games like Dark Souls, Elden Ring, and similar titles have taught me resilience through repeated failure—a lesson directly applicable to debugging production systems.

Beyond entertainment, gaming has sharpened my strategic thinking, hand-eye coordination, and ability to adapt to complex systems with multiple moving parts. The competitive aspects of gaming have also fostered a community-oriented mindset and the importance of teamwork and communication. Playing with friends across different time zones and locations keeps me connected and provides a healthy outlet for stress relief after intense work days.

Gaming has influenced my career perspective in unexpected ways—I've learned to appreciate elegant system design, smooth user experience, and the importance of feedback loops in complex interactive systems. Whether it's optimizing performance, balancing difficulty curves, or designing intuitive interfaces, the principles I see in well-crafted games directly inform my approach to building reliable, maintainable software systems.
